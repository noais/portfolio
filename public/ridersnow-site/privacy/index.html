Privacy Policy

Last updated: 12 May 2026

RidersNow ("we", "the app") is built and operated by David Vicente Ferreira. This policy explains what we collect, why, and what your rights are. We try to keep it short and human.

1. What we collect

Account information

When you sign in with Apple or Google, we receive your provider user ID, your display name (if you share it), your email address, and — if you signed in with Google — an avatar URL. We store these so you can sign in again and so other riders can recognise you.

Profile content

Information you add yourself: display name, bio, sports, gear, an avatar photo, a phone number for WhatsApp deep-links (optional), and country.

Location

When you create a check-in, post a session, or activate Host mode, we store the coordinates you chose for that activity. We do not track your location in the background and we do not store your live device location unless you have an active check-in. You can end a check-in at any time, which removes you from the live map immediately.

Activity

The sessions you create, check-ins you start, planned sessions you join, hosts you message — all kept so the app works as a tool. You can delete your account at any time, which removes this data.

Push notification tokens

If you grant push permission, your Apple Push Notification Service device token is stored against your profile so we can deliver notifications about your sessions and check-ins.

Analytics & crash reports (opt-in)

During onboarding we ask whether you want to share anonymous product analytics. If you opt in, we record:

  • App lifecycle events (opened, closed, screen views) via PostHog (EU region).
  • Anonymous crash reports via Sentry.

Analytics is identified by your Supabase user ID, never by email or name. We strip personally identifying fields (email, phone, exact coordinates, display name) from analytics payloads. You can change your mind any time from Settings.

Crash reports are collected even if you decline analytics, but contain no personally identifying information — only technical state needed to fix the crash.

2. What we do not collect

  • We do not sell your data.
  • We do not track you across other apps or the web.
  • We do not run third-party advertising SDKs.
  • We do not record your location unless you have an active check-in or are an active Host.

3. Who can see what

  • Your check-in pin (location, sport, gear) is visible to other riders on the map only while the check-in is active.
  • Your planned-session post (location, sport, time, your display name) is visible to other riders until you delete it.
  • Your Host pin is visible only while Host mode is on.
  • Your phone / WhatsApp number is shared with another rider only when one of you confirms participation in a session you created or joined.

4. Where data lives

Data is stored in Supabase (PostgreSQL, EU region). Push tokens are sent to Apple Push Notification Service for delivery. Analytics goes to PostHog (EU region). Crash reports go to Sentry.

5. Your rights

You can:

  • Edit your profile from Settings.
  • End a check-in or delete a planned session at any time.
  • Delete your account from Settings — this removes your profile, sessions, check-ins, hosts and participations from our database.
  • Email us at david.vicenteferreira@gmail.com with any request (data export, correction, deletion).

6. Children

RidersNow is intended for ages 13+. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13.

7. Changes

We'll update this page if anything changes. Material changes will be highlighted in the app.
